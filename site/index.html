Epitaxial Deposition of Ga2O3 using mist-CVD

Wide band gap oxides have potential application in areas of power electronics and deep UV-optielectronics, recently Ga2O3 sesquioxide has emerged as semiconductor of choice for aformentioned applications. A good talk on its usability as a material for FET can be found at β-Ga2O3 FET. Ga2O3 exhibit five polymorphs α-, β-, γ-, ε/κ- and δ ; β-Ga2O3 being the most stable phase among all and hence most research was focussed on the same. β-Ga2 was grown using techniques:

  1. MBE
  2. CVD
    • MOCVD
    • mist-CVD
  3. MOVPE
  4. ALD
  5. PLD
  6. Microwave-assisted
  7. EFG
  8. Float Zone
β-Ga2O3 has an ultra wide band gap of 4.8 eV and crystallizes in monoclinic crystal structure (see Figure 2). The very assymetric phase of the same is responsible for higly assymetric electronic and optical properties; also alloying β-Ga2O3 with Al to tune its bandgap for deep UV optoelectronics is limited to Al mole fraction of ∼ 33 %. This motivates to investigate into other metastable polymorphs of Ga2O3.
